The Guardians

Craftsmanship built on
obsession.

We are not just builders; we are guests in your sanctuary. Sage & Stone was founded on a simple rebellion: that a kitchen should be built to outlive its owner.

Meet the Makers
Thomas J. Wright Master Joiner & Founder

"I was tired of seeing kitchens designed to last a warranty, not a lifetime."

Twenty years ago, I walked onto a job site in London. I saw "luxury" cabinets being unloaded—they were stapled MDF wrapped in a veneer that would peel before the decade was out. They were expensive, but they weren't valuable.

I started Sage & Stone to fight the disposable culture of our industry. We don't chase trends because good design doesn't expire. We build the way they used to: with solid timber, proper joinery, and an obsessive attention to the parts you don't see.

When we hand over the keys, I put my name on the frame. That isn't branding. That is a personal guarantee that this room will serve your great-grandchildren.

Thomas J. Wright
Etiquette & Standards

Respect for the Sanctuary

We understand that we are working in your home, not just a building site. Our reputation is built on how we leave the room, not just how we build it.

01.

The Invisible Tradesmen

Noise is managed. Music is forbidden. We aim to be felt in the quality of the finish, but not heard during the process. We interact politely with neighbors and ensure parking is discreet.

02.

Obsessive Cleanliness

We don't leave a mess for "tomorrow." The site is cleaned down every single evening. You should never come home to dust. We treat your floors and art with the same care we treat our timber.

03.

Vetted & Permanent

We do not pick up subcontractors on the morning of the job. Every member of the Sage & Stone team is fully employed, vetted, and trained in our specific code of conduct. You will know their names.
