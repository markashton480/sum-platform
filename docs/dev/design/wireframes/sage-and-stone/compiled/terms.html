Terms of Supply

We believe the paperwork should be as crafted as the joinery. Below is the framework of our agreement, designed to protect both the integrity of your home and the quality of our work.

Last Updated: October 2025

1. Definitions

In this Agreement, the following terms shall have the following meanings:

  • "The Maker" refers to Sage & Stone Ltd, registered in England & Wales (No. 12345678).
  • "The Client" refers to the person(s) or entity named on the Quotation.
  • "The Commission" refers to the bespoke joinery, cabinetry, and installation works specified in the signed Proposal.

2. Scope of Works

In Plain English

We build what is on the quote. We don't paint walls, rewire your whole house, or remove asbestos unless we've specifically agreed to it in writing. If you change your mind halfway through, the price will likely change too.

2.1. The Maker shall provide the goods and services explicitly detailed in the final approved Design Proposal and Quotation.

2.2. Exclusions: Unless explicitly itemized in the Quotation, the following are excluded from The Commission:

  • Final decoration of walls, ceilings, or existing joinery not supplied by The Maker.
  • Structural building works, plastering, or making good of uneven floors/walls prior to installation.
  • Connection of gas appliances (must be performed by a Gas Safe registered engineer).
  • Removal of hazardous materials (e.g., asbestos).

2.3. Any variations to the design requested by The Client after the "Sign-Off" date will be treated as a Variation Order and may incur additional costs and extend the Timeline.

3. Payment Structure

To secure workshop time and materials, payments are structured as follows:

  • Stage 1: 40%
    The Booking Deposit

    Due upon acceptance of the Quote. This secures your slot in our production schedule and allows us to procure raw timber.

  • Stage 2: 40%
    The Production Payment

    Due 4 weeks prior to the installation date. This covers the intensive labor period in the workshop.

  • Stage 3: 20%
    The Completion Balance

    Due within 7 days of practical completion of the installation works.

3.1. Late payments may result in the suspension of works. Sage & Stone reserves the right to charge interest on overdue amounts at 3% above the Bank of England base rate.

3.2. Title to all goods remains with The Maker until the final balance is paid in full.

4. Living Materials

In Plain English

Wood moves. Brass tarnishes. Marble stains. This is not a defect; it is character. We use natural materials that will age and season over time, just like your home.

4.1. The Client acknowledges that wood, stone, and unlacquered brass are natural materials. Variations in grain, color, and texture are inherent and are not grounds for rejection.

4.2. Acclimatization: Timber reacts to humidity. While we kiln-dry our timber to optimal moisture content, minor movement (expansion/contraction) may occur as the furniture acclimatizes to your home's central heating. This is normal behavior for solid wood joinery.

4.3. Patina: Finishes specified as "Living" (e.g., Unlacquered Brass, Oiled Oak) will change appearance over time based on use and environment.

5. Access & Logistics

5.1. The Client agrees to provide The Maker with clear access to the property between the hours of 08:00 and 17:00, Monday to Friday, for the duration of the installation.

5.2. The installation area must be clear of other trades, furniture, and debris before our arrival. Delays caused by lack of site readiness may incur "Waiting Time" charges.

5.3. Force Majeure: The Maker shall not be liable for delays caused by events outside our reasonable control, including but not limited to: supplier failure, extreme weather, or acts of God.

6. The Guarantee

Lifetime Joinery Guarantee

We build for the next generation. Sage & Stone guarantees the structural integrity of our cabinetry for the lifetime of the original ownership.

What is Covered
  • Structural failure of cabinet carcasses.
  • Failure of door hinges or drawer runners (Blum/Grass mechanisms).
  • Detachment of face frames.
What is Not Covered
  • Accidental damage or misuse.
  • Water damage caused by plumbing leaks.
  • Normal wear and tear to painted finishes.
